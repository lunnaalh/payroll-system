What Does Clean Up Mean?

"Clean-up" is a collective effort to remove trash from natural environments like beaches, rivers, and forests — reducing pollution's harmful impact on wildlife and ecosystems. In places like Bali, where plastic waste threatens marine life, clean-ups restore beauty and inspire change.

More than just picking up litter, clean-up initiatives raise awareness and encourage responsible waste management. Every piece of trash removed is a step toward a cleaner future — a movement for a healthier planet, a commitment to change, and a journey back to our blue ocean.

The organizations below can help us on our journey toward an eco-friendly world through community-driven clean-up initiatives and sustainable environmental solutions!

Partner Organizations

Trash Hero

Trash Hero is a global volunteer movement dedicated to reducing waste through community clean-ups, education, and sustainable solutions. Founded in 2013, it operates in multiple countries including Indonesia, inspiring thousands to take action toward responsible waste management and plastic reduction.

Learn More

Sungai Watch

Sungai Watch is an environmental organization dedicated to protecting Indonesia's rivers from plastic pollution. Founded by the Bencheghib siblings, they install floating barriers to intercept waste before it reaches the ocean, while also organizing clean-ups, recycling initiatives, and community education programs across Bali.
